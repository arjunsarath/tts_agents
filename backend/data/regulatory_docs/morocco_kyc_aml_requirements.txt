Morocco KYC/AML Requirements for Remittance Operators

Source: Bank Al-Maghrib (BAM) Circular 5/W/2017, Morocco Anti-Money Laundering Law 43-05

REGULATORY AUTHORITY
Bank Al-Maghrib (BAM) is the primary regulatory authority overseeing money transfer and remittance services in Morocco. The Financial Intelligence Unit (Unité de Traitement du Renseignement Financier - UTRF) handles suspicious activity reports.

LICENSING REQUIREMENTS
- Money transfer operators must obtain authorization from Bank Al-Maghrib
- Operators must demonstrate adequate capital requirements (minimum MAD 2 million for domestic operators)
- Foreign operators must partner with licensed Moroccan financial institutions or obtain a specific license
- Annual compliance audits are mandatory

CUSTOMER IDENTIFICATION (KYC)
Under Bank Al-Maghrib Circular 5/W/2017:

For individuals:
- Full legal name as it appears on official identification
- Date of birth
- Nationality
- Valid government-issued photo ID (Carte Nationale d'Identité for Moroccan nationals, passport for foreign nationals)
- Residential address (proof may be required for transactions exceeding MAD 20,000)
- Professional activity/occupation

For legal entities:
- Company registration documents (Registre du Commerce)
- Articles of association
- Identity of beneficial owners (25% or more ownership threshold)
- Tax identification number
- Registered office address

TRANSACTION THRESHOLDS AND LIMITS
- Individual transfers up to MAD 20,000: Standard KYC verification
- Transfers exceeding MAD 20,000: Enhanced due diligence required
- Transfers exceeding MAD 50,000: Source of funds documentation required
- Annual cumulative limit of MAD 200,000 for occasional customers
- No specific per-transaction limit for established business relationships with adequate KYC

AML OBLIGATIONS
Under Morocco's Anti-Money Laundering Law 43-05:
- Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs) must be filed with UTRF within 30 days of detection
- Cash transactions exceeding MAD 150,000 must be reported
- Record keeping for a minimum of 10 years for all customer identification documents
- Record keeping for a minimum of 10 years for all transaction records
- Risk-based approach to customer due diligence
- Enhanced due diligence for politically exposed persons (PEPs)
- Ongoing monitoring of business relationships

FOREIGN EXCHANGE CONTROLS
- Morocco maintains foreign exchange controls managed by the Office des Changes
- Inbound remittances are generally unrestricted
- Outbound transfers by Moroccan residents are subject to annual limits
- Moroccan diaspora benefit from specific foreign exchange advantages (Compte en Dirhams Convertibles)

CORRIDORS
Morocco is a major recipient of remittances, particularly from:
- France (largest corridor)
- Spain
- Italy
- United Kingdom
- Belgium
- Netherlands
- United Arab Emirates
- Canada
- United States

RECENT REGULATORY DEVELOPMENTS (2023-2024)
- Bank Al-Maghrib has been strengthening digital payment regulations
- New guidelines for mobile money and electronic payment services
- Enhanced cross-border payment monitoring
- Morocco's ongoing efforts to align with FATF recommendations
- Focus on beneficial ownership transparency
- Digital identity verification methods becoming accepted for lower-risk transactions

PENALTIES
Non-compliance with AML/KYC requirements may result in:
- Administrative fines up to MAD 1,000,000
- Suspension or revocation of operating license
- Criminal penalties including imprisonment for serious violations
- Enhanced supervisory measures and increased reporting requirements
